We presented a systematic comparison of four variational quantum circuit
architectures---fully-connected (FC-VQC), residual (ResNet-VQC), hybrid
quantum transformer (QT), and fully quantum transformer (FQT)---on five
tabular benchmarks.

Our central finding is that \textbf{simpler is better}: multi-layer FC-VQCs
achieve the strongest accuracy-to-parameter ratio, matching gradient-boosted
trees on regression tasks with ${\sim}$700 parameters.  Quantum
self-attention, despite its conceptual appeal, does not consistently improve
performance on small tabular data and can even hurt it.  Expressibility
analysis confirms that shallow circuits (depth~${\approx}\,3$) already
saturate the accessible Hilbert space, explaining why deep or complex
architectures provide diminishing returns.

These results provide actionable guidance for the quantum ML community:
FC-VQCs should be the default architecture for tabular tasks on near-term
hardware, with ResNet-VQC as a robust alternative.  Transformer-style VQCs
may find their niche on structured, sequential, or high-dimensional data
where attention mechanisms have proven beneficial in the classical setting,
but this remains a direction for future work.

Future directions include validation on real quantum hardware, scaling to
larger qubit counts and higher-dimensional datasets, and exploring quantum
attention on sequential tasks (e.g.\ time-series forecasting) where the
inductive bias of self-attention may be better matched to the data structure.
